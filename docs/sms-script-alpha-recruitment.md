## Sending Guidelines

### When to use SMS
- **Best case:** You already have the operator's phone number from a previous conversation or mutual contact
- **Good case:** SMEA member directory lists phone numbers — use cautiously
- **Avoid:** Buying phone lists or scraping numbers — that's spam, not outreach

### Who to contact first
1. **Referrals** — operators who referred others already on the target list
2. **Known contacts** — people Tye has met at events, truck stops, depots
3. **SMEA members** — only if phone numbers are publicly listed or provided through a member directory

### What to track per SMS
- Number sent
- Date/time sent
- Response (YES / NO / no reply / wrong number)
- If YES → send SMS Script 4 immediately with the URL

### Opt-out compliance
- Always include "Unsubscribe: reply STOP" 
- If someone replies STOP → do NOT send further messages, note in tracking sheet

---

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Keep it under 3 sentences
- Lead with the problem (spreadsheets) not the solution
- Make the ask clear: "Test it, give feedback, get free access"
- Include your name and company upfront
- Send during business hours (6am-6pm local time)

### Don't
- Don't send after hours (operators may be on the road or at rest stops)
- Don't include links in the first message — wait for them to say YES
- Don't use abbreviations operators won't understand
- Don't promise things the alpha can't deliver ("real-time", "live prices")
- Don't send more than 2 messages without a reply
