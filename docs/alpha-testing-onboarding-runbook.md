# Alpha Tester Onboarding Runbook

**Date:** 2026-07-15
**Owner:** Tye (Marketing)
**Purpose:** Step-by-step process for onboarding 5 real operators to test the quote calculator.

## Overview
- **Goal:** Get 5 operators to complete a full quote end-to-end and provide structured feedback
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks (contact → onboarding → testing → feedback)
- **Resource:** 1 person (Marketing) for outreach and interviews

## Stage 1: Outreach (Days 1-7)

### Step 1: Send initial outreach emails
- Use template from `outreach-templates.md`
- Send to target contacts (see `alpha-testing-target-list.md`)
- Post in Facebook groups (requires chair's Facebook access)
- **Goal:** Get 10+ responses (assume 20% reply rate from 50+ contacts)

### Step 2: Confirm availability
- When someone replies, confirm:
  - Do they currently use spreadsheets/paper for quoting?
  - Are they available for a 30-minute testing session (phone call or in-person)?
  - What trucking/earthmoving work do they do? (industry, state, fleet size)
- **Goal:** 5 confirmed testers

### Step 3: Send test invitation
- Email each confirmed tester with:
  - The calculator URL (once deployed)
  - Instructions: "Open on your phone, enter a real job route, tell us if the numbers look right"
  - Calendar invite for the feedback interview (scheduled within 3 days)
  - What to bring: A recent job they've quoted (distance, fuel type, equipment, labour)

## Stage 2: Testing Session (Days 8-14)

### Step 4: Welcome email (Day 8)
**Subject:** Your Truk calculator test — thanks for jumping in!

G'day [Name],

Thanks for agreeing to test our quote calculator. Here's what you need to know:

**What to do before our session:**
- Open the calculator at [URL] on your phone
- Enter a real job you've recently quoted (distance, fuel consumption, diesel price)
- See what the calculator gives you
- Think about: Does this look right? Is it faster than your current method? What's missing?

**Our 30-minute session:**
- [Date/Time]
- [Phone call or in-person — TBD]
- We'll walk through the calculator together
- I'll ask you some questions — there's no wrong answer
- Honest feedback is the only thing we want

You can use this job as your test case:
- Distance: 500 km
- Consumption: 35 L/100km
- Diesel price: $1.80/L (or your local price)
- Equipment: [their actual equipment]
- Labour rate: $[their rate]

Cheers,
Tye

### Step 5: Conduct feedback interview
- Use structured interview template (see below)
- Record notes (or get chair permission to record audio)
- Watch them use the calculator — observe, don't just ask
- Note: mobile usability, accuracy confidence, time saved, missing fields, willingness to pay

### Step 6: Collect written feedback
- After the session, email a short survey:
  - Did the calculator give you confidence in your fuel cost quotes? (Yes/No/Not sure)
  - How long did it take to enter a full quote? (estimate)
  - What would you change? (open text)
  - Would you pay $X/month for this? (Yes/No/Depends)

## Stage 3: Wrap-Up (Days 15-21)

### Step 7: Send thank-you note
- Acknowledge their time
- Offer continued access to beta
- Ask for referrals: "Do you know another operator who'd be interested?"

### Step 8: Compile feedback reports
- One report per tester (see feedback report template)
- Summary validation report with aggregate findings

## Structured Interview Template

### Accuracy Confidence
1. When you entered [their real job], did the fuel cost the calculator gave you look right to you? Why or why not?
2. On a scale of 1-5, how confident would you feel showing this quote to a customer? (1 = not confident, 5 = very confident)
3. What specific fields would you check or adjust before quoting?

### Time Saved
4. How long does it currently take you to quote this job? (spreadsheet/paper)
5. How long did it take with the calculator?
6. Is the time saved worth it? Why or why not?

### Mobile Usability
7. How easy was it to use on your phone? (1-5)
8. Were there any fields hard to find or hard to enter?
9. Did you run into any confusion or errors?

### Missing Fields
10. What information do you need for a quote that the calculator doesn't ask for?
11. Is there anything you always include in quotes that's missing here?
12. If you could add one field, what would it be?

### Willingness to Pay
13. We're thinking of charging a flat monthly fee for this. Would you pay for it?
14. If yes, what's a fair price? (suggest $99-199/month range)
15. What would make you cancel? (churn triggers)

### Overall
16. What's the #1 thing that would make this indispensable to you?
17. Would you recommend this to another operator? Why or why not?

## Known Risks
- Operators may not have time for a 30-minute session
- May need to offer a phone call instead of in-person
- Some operators may be uncomfortable with their phone as primary device
- Response rate may be lower than 20% — need more outreach
