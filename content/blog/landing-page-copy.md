## Tone Guidelines for Developer Integration

1. **Short paragraphs.** No wall of text. Mobile-first means 1-2 sentences per paragraph max.
2. **Australian English.** "Programme" → "program", "realise" → "realise", " litre" not "liter".
3. **No corporate speak.** No "synergy", "solution", "optimize", "empower", "seamless". Operators smell that from a kilometre away.
4. **No over-promising.** If a feature isn't live yet (e.g. multi-state expansion), don't claim it is. Say "across Australian states" — not "every state today".
5. **Numbers are sourced.** Pricing ($50 Starter, $150 Pro) is from the authoritative pricing document (cmpnbp62l). Fuel API claim is from FEATURES.pdf (cmpq434s0). All validated.
6. **"Live diesel prices" — NOT "real-time".** The data source (FEATURES.pdf, blog post) says "live diesel prices" and "updated daily". "Real-time" over-promises. Use "live" when referring to diesel price freshness. The company goal is "Real-time Fuel Data Accuracy" but that's an aspirational goal, not a current feature description.
6. **CTAs are action-oriented.** "Calculate Your Fuel Costs", "Start Your Free Trial". Not "Learn More" or "Get Started".

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- ❌ "From $60/month" — incorrect, it's $50 (per: Marketing Pricing Summary)
- ❌ "One flat fee, one price" — incorrect, there are 3 tiers
- ❌ "Starting at $180" — incorrect, Pro is $150
- ❌ "Freemium" — we have a trial, not a free tier
- ❌ "Per-truck fees" / "per-seat pricing" — never true, never say it
- ❌ "Best value" — subjective, not actionable
- ❌ "Real-time" claims on features not yet live — check before writing
